Bioengineering

My name is Venkatesh Tavva and I am currently a graduate student in the Bioengineering Department at the University of California, Riverside. For undergraduate, I attended University of California, Riverside, where I graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Bioengineering.

I am deeply interested in the intersection between medicine and engineering as this allows for the creation of new technologies and products that were never thought possible with the aim of not only improving peoples lives, but also ultimately changing lives for the better. Through bioengineering, I have been exposed to many issues that are in need of solving and have been taught the fundamentals through which we can approach these problems. I enjoy the problem-solving aspect of bioengineering and it is because of this keen interest, I am leaning forward towards pursuing a career in Research and Development.

As far as experience is concerned, I have worked in the Ghosh lab, as a Undergraduate Researcher. It is here where I was exposed many fundamnetal techniques used in wet labs for experiments, as well as creating experiments for finding answers to small questions that would eventually lead us to attaining a better picture of the potential solution and the means through which we can find that solution.
